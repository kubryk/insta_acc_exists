<p style="text-align: left;">Fulani braids are a great way to style your hair. So, if you are searching for daring yet beautiful braided hairstyles, look no more, here they are!<!--more--></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Let’s start with the question: "what are Fulani braids?" It is a distinctive hairstyle widely spread in African communities and named after the Fula ethnic group. It is characterized by exceptional patterns of thin or medium tightly plaited braids that stick closely to the head.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Many women worldwide prefer authentic Fulani braids, also known as Fulani tribal braids, as a way to express themselves. Alicia Keys, for example, is one of the all-time braids' lovers among celebrities. Besides, different Fulani braids styles often involve colorful extensions and various decorative elements, for instance, beads, rings, and chains.</p>

<p style="text-align: left;"><strong><center>Fulani Braids Q&A </center></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Here are a few answers to the most frequently asked questions about Fulani braids.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>1. How long do Fulani braids last?</strong> If you take good care of your braids, they will last up to five weeks before a correction is required.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>2. How much do Fulani braids cost?</strong> The cost of Fulani braids depends on the style significantly. However, the average price is $200.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>3. Do Fulani braids damage your hair?</strong> Unless you plait them too tight, those braids are even good for hair as they provide them some rest.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>4. How to care for Fulani braids?</strong> Wash them less often - once every two weeks. Protect them during the night, avoid tightening hairdos, and redo the edges when necessary.</p>
